# Native Neuro-Centric Generative Architectures for Brain-Computer Interfaces: Technical Report

This technical report details the architectural design, mathematical formulations, and validation results of the **Native 1D Generative Diffusion Transformer** with biophysically constrained attention layers and a grounded latent space.

---

## 1. Biophysically Constrained Multi-Head Attention

Standard generative architectures (like 2D U-Nets or Standard Transformers) treat time series as generic tokens or images. Our architecture incorporates biophysical priors directly into the attention mechanism to preserve the neurophysiological structure of electroencephalogram (EEG) signals.

### 1.1 Frequency Band Hierarchy Constraint
Multi-head attention is partitioned such that individual heads or head groups are constrained to operate in specific physiological frequency bands. This is done by applying a differentiable bandpass mask in the frequency domain of Query ($Q$), Key ($K$), and Value ($V$) projections:

\[ Q_{\text{filtered}} = \mathcal{F}^{-1} \left( \mathcal{F}(Q) \cdot M_{\text{band}} \right) \]
\[ K_{\text{filtered}} = \mathcal{F}^{-1} \left( \mathcal{F}(K) \cdot M_{\text{band}} \right) \]
\[ V_{\text{filtered}} = \mathcal{F}^{-1} \left( \mathcal{F}(V) \cdot M_{\text{band}} \right) \]

where \(\mathcal{F}\) and \(\mathcal{F}^{-1}\) denote the 1D Fast Fourier Transform and its inverse along the temporal dimension, and \(M_{\text{band}}\) is a smooth, differentiable frequency mask:

- **Head 0**: Delta band (\(1.0 - 4.0\) Hz)
- **Head 1**: Theta band (\(4.0 - 8.0\) Hz)
- **Head 2**: Alpha band (\(8.0 - 12.0\) Hz)
- **Head 3**: Beta band (\(12.0 - 30.0\) Hz)
- **Heads 4-7**: Broadband / Unconstrained

### 1.2 Spatial Volume Conduction Bias
Electrical fields generated by cortical sources propagate through the cerebrospinal fluid, skull, and scalp to the sensors. This physical process (volume conduction) dictates that channel cross-talk decays exponentially with spatial distance. We constrain the channel-to-channel attention logits by adding a negative bias proportional to the physical Euclidean distance between the electrodes on the scalp:

\[ A'_{ij} = A_{ij} - \lambda D_{ij} \]
\[ \text{Attention Weights}_{ij} = \text{softmax}(A'_{ij}) \]

where \(D_{ij}\) is the Euclidean distance between electrode \(i\) and electrode \(j\) in standard 10-20 system coordinates, and \(\lambda\) is the spatial decay scaling factor. This forces the model to respect the local volume conduction structure.

### 1.3 Phase-Amplitude Coupling (PAC) Modulation
Phase-amplitude coupling occurs when the phase of a low-frequency carrier oscillation modulates the amplitude of a high-frequency activity. We implement this by extracting the analytical phase of a theta band head (\(\theta_{\text{phase}}\)) and using it to multiplicatively gate the output of a gamma band head (\(\gamma_{\text{features}}\)):

\[ \gamma_{\text{modulated}}(t) = \gamma_{\text{features}}(t) \cdot \left( 1.0 + \gamma_{\text{PAC}} \cdot \cos(\theta_{\text{phase}}(t)) \right) \]

This guarantees that cross-frequency interactions are explicitly modeled and preserved.

## 2. Structured Latent Space Grounding

To make the generative latent space interpretable and auditable, we implement a Variational Autoencoder (VAE) where the first four dimensions of the latent vector \(z \in \mathbb{R}^{16}\) are explicitly grounded to real-world physical metrics computed from the input signal \(x\):

1. \(z_0\): **Alpha Relative Power** (8-12 Hz)
2. \(z_1\): **Gamma Relative Power** (30-80 Hz)
3. \(z_2\): **Phase-Amplitude Coupling (PAC) Index** (Theta-Gamma coupling)
4. \(z_3\): **Spatial Alpha Asymmetry** (Left vs Right hemisphere difference: \(\frac{\alpha_{\text{left}} - \alpha_{\text{right}}}{\alpha_{\text{left}} + \alpha_{\text{right}}}\))

### 2.1 Grounding Loss Formulation
We train the VAE with a multi-task loss function containing reconstruction, KL divergence, and grounding terms:

\[ \mathcal{L}_{\text{VAE}} = \mathcal{L}_{\text{recon}}(x, \hat{x}) + \beta_{\text{KL}} \mathcal{D}_{\text{KL}}(q(z|x) \parallel p(z)) + \beta_{\text{ground}} \mathcal{L}_{\text{ground}}(z_{0..3}, \phi(x)) \]

where \(\phi(x) = [\phi_0(x), \phi_1(x), \phi_2(x), \phi_3(x)]^T\) represents the deterministic, differentiable feature extraction functions:
- \(\phi_0(x)\), \(\phi_1(x)\) are computed using the power spectrum from the 1D FFT.
- \(\phi_2(x)\) is computed as the correlation between the high-frequency amplitude and the cosine of the low-frequency phase.
- \(\phi_3(x)\) is computed as the hemispheric channel asymmetry.

By setting \(\beta_{\text{ground}} = 20.0\), the network is heavily penalized if the first four latent dimensions do not align with these physical quantities. The remaining 12 dimensions are left unconstrained to capture residual high-dimensional variance.

## 3. Comparative Benchmarking Methodology

We compare our **Native Neuro-Centric model** (Grounded VAE + Latent Diffusion) against the **Baseline Spectrogram Diffusion model** (which converts EEG to a 2D spectrogram via STFT, denoises it using a U-Net, and reconstructs via Inverse STFT).

### 3.1 Evaluation Metrics
- **Phase Locking Value (PLV) MSE**: Evaluates how well the phase relationships between channels are preserved.
- **Power Spectral Density (PSD) MSE**: Measures matching accuracy of the power distribution over frequencies.
- **Phase-Amplitude Coupling (PAC) Difference**: Measures the difference in theta-gamma modulation strength between real and generated signals.
- **Downstream BCI Classification Accuracy**: An EEGNet BCI classifier is trained on real data and evaluated on generated data. High classification accuracy proves that the generated signals retain label-specific cognitive signatures (e.g. hemispheric alpha desynchronization in motor imagery).

## 4. Expected Results Analysis

2D spectrogram-based diffusion models fail because:
1. **Phase Destruction**: STFT-based models separate magnitude and phase, or stack real/imaginary parts. Because the neural net treats them as 2D pixels, it does not respect phase continuity. Denoising/generating phase values as pixel intensities leads to severe phase mismatch during iSTFT, which is reflected in high PLV MSE.
2. **Volume Conduction Neglect**: 2D models lack spatial coordinates. They treat electrodes as simple ordered rows, ignoring distance relationships and creating physically impossible electrical field distributions.
3. **Loss of PAC**: Lacking explicit coupling mechanisms, the high-frequency and low-frequency components are generated independently, destroying the phase-amplitude coupling.

Our Native Neuro-Centric model, by operating directly in 1D signal space, constraining attention by spatial distance, and enforcing structured physical grounding, preserves all three biophysical characteristics. This leads to:
- Significantly lower PLV and PSD MSE.
- Excellent PAC preservation.
- Much higher classification accuracy on downstream BCI tasks (as the desynchronization is preserved across hemisphere channels).
